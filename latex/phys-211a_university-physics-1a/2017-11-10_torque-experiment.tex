Find a formula for \( I \) in terms of \( \alpha \).
\begin{align*}
  \Delta y &= s = R\theta \quad F_{net} = ma \quad \tau_{net} = I\alpha \\
  -\frac{I\alpha}{R}-mg &= ma = mR\alpha \\
  -\frac{I\alpha}{R}-mR\alpha &= mg \\
  -\frac{I\alpha}{R} &= mg+mR\alpha \\
  I &= -\frac{R}{\alpha}(mg+mR\alpha) \\
  &= -\frac{mgR}{\alpha}-mR^2 \\
  &= -Rm(\frac{g}{\alpha}+R)
\end{align*}
